Bay Area Jams Page



It is with sad regret that I have indefinitely abandoned this project. I have found it too difficult to keep track of jam sessions as they open and close. Perhaps it is the current economic climate, but few if any jam sessions are constant enough to be usefully listed here. I deeply wish that San Francisco could support a steady and lively jam seen (especially in jazz, which is my focus) but it seems not to be the case at the moment. Thank you for those of you that have contributed and offered your encouragement. Maybe in the future if things stabilize, I can try it again. Until then, please consider these listings:

Welcome,

This page is intended to be a listing of jam session in the San Francsico Bay Area. I have seen other pages like this, but this one is by a musician, for musicians. Only jam sessions that meet on a regular, predictable basis (at lease once a month) and are open to the general public and are open to outside players (as opposed to private jams) will be listed. If you find any errors, please feel free to email me and let me know. If you are submitting a jam session for listing, please try to include the following information: venue, day, time, cost, city, address, phone, contact person, type of music, skill level, equipment available, and any other pertinent information. If you have more information about a listed jam session, please email me.

I define a jam session as musicians (who may or may not know each other) coming together to play unrehearsed music. This would not include bands that "jam" or even invite a few friends to sit-it. I will be focussing on jams that involve improvisation.

Just having the word "jam" in the title is not enough nor is the fact that you and your buddies "jam out" on stage. The house band may play the first set by themselves and still meet my definition of a jam session.

I have purposely chosen not to include "open mics" in the list. As an instrumentalist, my main focus is instrumental music. Many open mics are a parade of cheesy singers, mediocre poetry, and are often not very musician friendly. (There are exceptions, of course, but this is my general observation.) Additionally, listing all the available open mics would clog up the list with a bunch of gigs that typically don't interest my target audience.
